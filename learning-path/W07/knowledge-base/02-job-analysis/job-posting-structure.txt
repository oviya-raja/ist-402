JOB POSTING STRUCTURE GUIDE
Job Fitment Analysis Agent - Knowledge Base

This guide explains how to extract and analyze information from job postings for fitment analysis.

JOB POSTING SECTIONS
====================

1. JOB TITLE
------------
What to Extract:
- Exact job title
- Level indicator (I, II, Senior, Principal, etc.)
- Department/Team (if mentioned)

Examples:
- "Software Engineer I"
- "Senior Software Engineer - Backend"
- "Associate Software Engineer"
- "Software Engineer - New Grad"

2. COMPANY INFORMATION
----------------------
What to Extract:
- Company name
- Department/Division
- Team/Group
- Location (city, state, country)
- Remote/Hybrid/On-site arrangement

3. JOB DESCRIPTION
-----------------
What to Extract:
- Role overview
- Key responsibilities
- Day-to-day activities
- Team structure
- Project types

4. REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS
--------------------------
Critical Section - Must-Have Requirements

What to Extract:
- Required skills (programming languages, frameworks, tools)
- Required experience level (years of experience)
- Required education (degree level, field of study)
- Required certifications
- Required work authorization

Format:
- List each requirement separately
- Note if it's "required" vs "must have"
- Identify skill proficiency level expected

Example Extraction:
Required Qualifications:
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field (REQUIRED)
- 2+ years of software development experience (REQUIRED)
- Proficiency in Python (REQUIRED)
- Experience with AWS (REQUIRED)
- Strong problem-solving skills (REQUIRED)

5. PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
---------------------------
Nice-to-Have Requirements

What to Extract:
- Preferred skills (bonus points)
- Preferred experience
- Preferred education
- Preferred certifications
- Nice-to-have attributes

Format:
- List each preference separately
- Note it's "preferred" or "nice to have"
- These add to fitment but aren't required

Example Extraction:
Preferred Qualifications:
- Master's degree in Computer Science (PREFERRED)
- Experience with Kubernetes (PREFERRED)
- Experience with microservices architecture (PREFERRED)
- Published research papers (PREFERRED)

6. LOCATION & WORK ARRANGEMENT
------------------------------
What to Extract:
- Office location (city, state)
- Remote work options
- Hybrid work arrangement
- Travel requirements
- Time zone requirements

Examples:
- "Remote - US"
- "San Francisco, CA (Hybrid - 3 days in office)"
- "On-site in Seattle, WA"
- "Remote or New York, NY"

7. SALARY & BENEFITS
--------------------
What to Extract (if available):
- Salary range
- Equity/Stock options
- Benefits package
- Perks

Note: Not always available, but useful for student decision-making

8. APPLICATION PROCESS
----------------------
What to Extract:
- Application deadline
- Interview process
- Required materials (resume, portfolio, etc.)

EXTRACTION METHODOLOGY
======================

Step 1: Identify Section Headers
---------------------------------
Look for common headers:
- "Requirements"
- "Qualifications"
- "What We're Looking For"
- "Required Skills"
- "Must Have"
- "Nice to Have"
- "Preferred"

Step 2: Categorize Requirements
--------------------------------
For each requirement, classify as:
- REQUIRED: Must-have, critical
- PREFERRED: Nice-to-have, bonus
- OPTIONAL: Mentioned but not emphasized

Step 3: Extract Skills
----------------------
For technical skills:
- Programming languages
- Frameworks
- Tools
- Platforms
- Databases
- Cloud services

For each skill, note:
- Proficiency level expected (if mentioned)
- Years of experience (if mentioned)
- Context of use (if mentioned)

Step 4: Extract Experience Requirements
----------------------------------------
Look for:
- "X+ years of experience"
- "Entry-level"
- "Mid-level"
- "Senior"
- "New grad"
- "Internship"

Step 5: Extract Education Requirements
---------------------------------------
Look for:
- Degree level (BS, MS, PhD)
- Field of study
- "or equivalent experience"
- Certification requirements

Step 6: Extract Location Information
--------------------------------------
Look for:
- City, state, country
- "Remote"
- "Hybrid"
- "On-site"
- "Flexible"

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
==================

For Fitment Analysis, organize extracted information:

REQUIRED SKILLS (Must Match):
- Skill 1: [extracted]
- Skill 2: [extracted]
- Skill 3: [extracted]

PREFERRED SKILLS (Bonus Points):
- Skill 1: [extracted]
- Skill 2: [extracted]

EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENT:
- Level: [Entry/Mid/Senior]
- Years: [X+ years]

EDUCATION REQUIREMENT:
- Degree: [BS/MS/PhD]
- Field: [Computer Science/related]

LOCATION:
- Arrangement: [Remote/Hybrid/On-site]
- Location: [City, State]

COMMON PATTERNS
===============

Pattern 1: Entry-Level Role
---------------------------
- "New grad" or "0-2 years experience"
- BS in CS or related
- Basic programming skills
- Willingness to learn

Pattern 2: Mid-Level Role
-------------------------
- "2-5 years experience"
- BS in CS (MS preferred)
- Specific technology stack
- Some leadership experience

Pattern 3: Senior Role
----------------------
- "5+ years experience"
- Deep expertise in specific area
- Leadership/mentoring experience
- System design experience

Pattern 4: Internship
---------------------
- Currently enrolled student
- Basic programming skills
- Eagerness to learn
- No experience required

EXAMPLE: COMPLETE EXTRACTION
=============================

Job Posting: Software Engineer I - Backend

EXTRACTED INFORMATION:

Job Title: Software Engineer I - Backend
Company: Tech Company Inc.
Location: Remote or San Francisco, CA

REQUIRED QUALIFICATIONS:
- Bachelor's degree in Computer Science or related field
- 1+ years of software development experience
- Proficiency in Python
- Experience with REST API development
- Experience with PostgreSQL or similar database
- Strong problem-solving skills

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS:
- Experience with AWS
- Experience with Docker
- Experience with microservices architecture
- Master's degree in Computer Science

EXPERIENCE REQUIREMENT:
- Level: Entry to Mid
- Years: 1+ years

EDUCATION REQUIREMENT:
- Degree: Bachelor's (Master's preferred)
- Field: Computer Science or related

LOCATION:
- Arrangement: Remote or Hybrid
- Location: San Francisco, CA

WORK ARRANGEMENT:
- Remote option available
- Hybrid option (3 days in office if in SF)
